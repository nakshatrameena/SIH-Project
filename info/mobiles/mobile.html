MOBILES
  • Copper, silver, gold, and palladium are a few precious metals present in smartphone devices. the total weight of precious metals in a smartphone typically falls within the range of 0.01% to 0.04% of the total weight
  • Li-ion batteries, microplastics, components made up of lead, mercury, and cadmium, and displays containing mercury and arsenic are some harmful components.
  • Smartphones contain hazardous materials, such as lead, mercury, and brominated flame retardants, which can leach into the environment.
  • The improper disposal or recycling of smartphones in developing countries can lead to pollution of soil and water, affecting both the environment and human health.
