Laptops
  • Copper, silver, gold, and palladium are a few precious metals present in laptop devices. The combined percentage of these precious metals in a laptop is typically less than 1% of the laptop's total weight
  • Lithium-ion or lithium-polymer batteries, LEDs and LCDs, plastics containing bisphenol A (BPA) or phthalates, and chemicals in their circuit boards, including lead and brominated flame retardants, are some harmful components.
  • Laptops and computers contain hazardous materials, such as lead, mercury, brominated flame retardants, and PVCs (polyvinyl chloride), which can leach into the environment if not disposed of or recycled correctly.
  • Toxic materials in laptops can pose health risks to workers and nearby communities. This exposure can lead to respiratory problems, neurological disorders, and other health issues.
